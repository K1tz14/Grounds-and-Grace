Our Heritage

A story steeped
in tradition

Born from a single vision — that coffee, like art, deserves reverence — Grounds & Grace opened its doors in 1987 in a converted Victorian bookshop. Our founder, Margaret Ellison, brought her grandmother's recipes from the highlands of Colombia and married them with the ritual of the English tea house.

Nearly four decades later, we still source single-origin beans by hand, roast in small batches every Thursday, and believe that the best cup you'll ever have is the one made for you personally.

The Art of the Pour

Every espresso pull is timed to the second. Our baristas train for six months before serving a single cup — not because we're perfectionists, but because you deserve nothing less.

02

Seasonal & Sourced

Our menu changes with the seasons and the harvest. We travel to origin farms in Ethiopia, Peru, and Sumatra to taste the beans before we buy, maintaining relationships built over decades.

03

The Space Itself

Designed around the rhythm of reading and conversation, our rooms hold only what's necessary — beautiful books, warm light, the smell of freshly ground beans, and room to breathe.

04

Community & Culture

Monthly cuppings, barista workshops, and an ongoing rotating gallery of local artists — because great coffee and great culture have always grown from the same soil.
